High property prices in coastal markets compress rent-to-value ratios, making it harder to achieve qualifying DSCR ratios. Inland markets offer stronger cash flow but different appreciation profiles. This guide breaks down the top California markets so you know where your DSCR deal actually works.

The Bay Area's extreme prices make DSCR qualification very difficult for standard purchase structures. Most deals require 35%+ down payments, ADU income inclusion, or multi-unit properties to achieve qualifying ratios. Despite this, the Bay Area has a significant DSCR loan market driven by tech workers who own multiple properties and can't qualify conventionally once they hit the 10-property cap or have complex income from RSUs and stock options.

The DSCR sweet spot for California investors in 2026: Sacramento and the Inland Empire offer the best risk-adjusted DSCR profile — strong ratios, growing rental demand, and meaningful appreciation potential without the pricing compression of coastal markets. If you're buying your first California investment property for cash flow, these are where to start.

Short-Term Rental (Airbnb) Markets — A Different DSCR Story

For STR-focused investors, the market analysis changes completely. Airbnb income in coastal California markets can be 3–5x long-term rental rates, completely transforming the DSCR calculation in markets that look terrible for long-term rentals.

Top California STR markets where DSCR loans work well using Airbnb income:

  • Palm Springs — peak season rates of $400–$800/night, strong year-round demand
  • Big Bear Lake — ski season drives premium winter rates
  • Lake Tahoe (CA side) — dual ski/summer season
  • Malibu / Laguna Beach — luxury coastal rates justify high purchase prices
  • Mammoth Lakes — year-round outdoor recreation demand

For STR DSCR loans, most lenders require either 12 months of Airbnb income history or a market rent analysis from AirDNA showing projected income at 75% occupancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a DSCR loan as an out-of-state investor buying in California?

Yes. DSCR loans qualify based on the property's income, not your location. Out-of-state investors commonly use DSCR to purchase California rental properties. Most lenders require a property management company for out-of-state borrowers.

Which California city has the best DSCR ratios?

San Bernardino, Riverside, and Sacramento consistently produce the best DSCR ratios in California due to their favorable rent-to-price ratios. Smaller cities in these counties often outperform the metro averages.

Can I use a DSCR loan for a California multi-family property?

Yes. Most DSCR lenders finance 2-4 unit properties. Some specialty lenders also do 5+ unit properties under DSCR guidelines. Multi-unit properties often produce better DSCR ratios than single-family homes in the same market due to higher combined rental income.
