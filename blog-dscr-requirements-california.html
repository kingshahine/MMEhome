DSCR Loan Requirements California 2026 — Everything You Need to Qualify

By Shane Bakhtiari, NMLS# 1082653 · Making Mortgage Easy · Updated May 2026

DSCR loans have far fewer requirements than conventional investment loans — but the requirements that exist are strictly enforced. This guide covers everything California investors need to know before applying in 2026.

Core Requirements at a Glance

DSCR Ratio0.75 minimum · 1.0+ preferred

Monthly rent ÷ monthly PITIA. Must be at least 0.75 with most lenders. Ratios of 1.0+ unlock the most lender options and best rates. Ratios of 1.25+ get the best pricing available.

Credit Score620 minimum · 680+ preferred

Most California DSCR lenders start at 640–680 minimum. Best rates at 740+. Unlike conventional loans, there's no DTI calculation — credit score is the primary borrower-level qualification factor.

Down Payment20% minimum · 25% for best rates

Minimum is typically 20% (80% LTV). Putting 25% down unlocks better rates and lower DSCR requirements with most lenders. Some California jumbo DSCR loans require 25–30% down.

Reserves6–12 months PITIA

After closing, lenders require 6–12 months of the property's total monthly payment in liquid assets. Can be bank accounts, retirement accounts (at 70% of value), or other liquid assets.

Property TypeSFR, 2-4 units, condos, STR

Single-family homes get the widest lender selection and best rates. 2-4 unit properties, warrantable condos, and short-term rentals are also eligible. Non-warrantable condos and rural properties have fewer options.

OccupancyInvestment property only

DSCR loans are for non-owner-occupied investment properties only. You cannot use a DSCR loan for your primary residence, second home, or any property you intend to occupy.

Documents You Need

This is where DSCR loans shine — the document list is dramatically shorter than conventional loans:

Required documents

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • 2 months recent bank statements (for down payment and reserves verification)
  • Executed lease agreement OR market rent analysis (for vacant properties)
  • Homeowners insurance quote for the property
  • Entity documents if vesting in LLC (Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, Certificate of Good Standing)
  • Property tax information
  • HOA documentation if applicable

What you do NOT need

  • W-2s or pay stubs
  • Personal tax returns (any year)
  • Business tax returns
  • Profit and loss statements
  • Employment verification letter
  • Bank statements showing income (only showing assets/reserves)

Self-employed investors: Your tax returns showing low income after deductions are completely irrelevant to DSCR qualification. The lender never sees them. Qualification is based entirely on the property's rental income and your credit score.

DSCR Requirements for Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb)

Short-term rental DSCR loans have slightly different requirements because income is not from a long-term lease:

  • Income documentation: 12 months of Airbnb/VRBO income statements OR an AirDNA market analysis showing projected income
  • Occupancy requirement: Most lenders use 75% of projected or actual STR income for qualification
  • Property must be legally permitted for short-term rental in the city/municipality
  • STR license or permit required in many California cities (Palm Springs, Mammoth, etc.)
  • Credit score typically higher: Most STR DSCR lenders require 680+ vs 640+ for long-term rentals

LLC Requirements for DSCR Loans

Most California investors want to hold investment properties in an LLC for liability protection. DSCR lenders accommodate this — unlike conventional lenders who require personal vesting. Here's what you need:

  • Articles of Organization (California LLC) or Articles of Incorporation
  • Operating Agreement showing ownership percentages
  • Certificate of Good Standing (issued within 90 days of closing)
  • EIN documentation
  • Personal guarantee from all members with 20%+ ownership interest
  • LLC bank account statements showing entity has been funded

Important: The personal guarantee means your personal credit still matters for DSCR loans in an LLC. Your personal income still does not. The lender will pull your personal credit and use your score to price the loan — but they won't verify your personal income.

Common Reasons DSCR Loans Get Declined in California

DSCR ratio too low

If rent doesn't cover at least 75% of the payment, most lenders won't proceed. Solutions: increase down payment, buy down the rate, find a higher-rent property, or use STR income projections if the market supports it.

Credit score below minimum

Below 620 and most DSCR lenders won't touch the file. Between 620–640, options are limited. Take 60–90 days to improve your score before applying if you're in this range.

Property condition issues

DSCR lenders won't approve properties in poor condition. The appraisal must confirm the property is habitable and in marketable condition. Significant deferred maintenance, structural issues, or unpermitted additions can kill a DSCR deal.

Short-term rental not legally permitted

Many California cities have banned or heavily restricted short-term rentals. If you're using Airbnb income for your DSCR calculation, the property must be legally permitted for STR in that jurisdiction. LA, San Francisco, and Santa Monica have strict STR regulations.

Insufficient reserves

Not having 6–12 months of PITIA in liquid assets after closing is a common stumbling block. Retirement accounts typically count at 70% of their value. Gift funds generally cannot be used for reserves.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do DSCR loans require an appraisal in California?

Yes. All DSCR loans require an appraisal to confirm property value and condition. The appraisal also includes a rent schedule (Form 1007) showing market rent for the property, which lenders use to verify the rental income used in the DSCR calculation.

Can I use rental income from other properties to qualify for a DSCR loan?

No. DSCR qualification is property-specific — only the income from the subject property (the one being financed) is used in the calculation. Income from your other properties doesn't factor into the DSCR ratio for a new purchase.

How long does a DSCR loan take to close in California?

Typically 14–21 days through wholesale lenders. The main variables are appraisal turnaround time and how quickly you provide required documents. Having all documents ready on day one is the biggest factor in fast closing.

Can I refinance a property I already own into a DSCR loan?

Yes. DSCR loans are available for rate/term refinance and cash-out refinance on existing investment properties. The same DSCR ratio requirements apply — the property's current rent must meet the minimum ratio at the new payment level.
