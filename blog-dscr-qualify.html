How to Qualify for a DSCR Loan — The Complete Guide

By Shane Bakhtiari, NMLS# 1082653 · Making Mortgage Easy · Updated April 2026

DSCR loans have become the go-to financing tool for real estate investors who are self-employed, have complex income, or are scaling beyond the conventional loan limits. This guide covers everything you need to know to qualify — from the ratio requirements to what documents you actually need.

The Core Requirements

DSCR Ratio0.75 minimum (1.0+ preferred)

The property's monthly rent divided by its monthly PITIA payment. Most lenders require 1.0+, but select lenders go down to 0.75 at slightly higher rates.

Credit Score620 minimum (680+ preferred)

Most lenders start at 640–680. The best rates require 720–740+. Unlike conventional loans, there's no DTI consideration — just the credit score and the property's cash flow.

Down Payment20–25% (varies by lender)

Most lenders require 20–25% down on DSCR loans. A higher down payment often unlocks better rates and lower DSCR requirements.

Reserves6–12 months PITIA

After closing, you need 6–12 months of the property's total payment in liquid reserves. This can be bank accounts, retirement accounts, or other liquid assets.

Property TypeSFR, 2-4 units, condos, STR

Single-family homes, 2-4 unit properties, condos, and short-term rentals all qualify. Some lenders also do 5+ unit commercial properties under DSCR guidelines.

Loan Amount$100,000 – $3,000,000+

DSCR loans go up to $3M+ through wholesale lenders. Jumbo DSCR loans above $1.5M have stricter requirements but are available.

What Documents Do You Actually Need?

This is where DSCR loans shine. The document list is dramatically shorter than conventional loans:

Required for all DSCR loans

  • Government-issued photo ID
  • 12 months bank statements (asset verification, not income verification)
  • Executed lease agreement OR market rent analysis (for vacant properties)
  • Property insurance quote
  • Entity documents (if vesting in LLC or corporation)

What you do NOT need

  • W-2s or pay stubs
  • Tax returns (personal or business)
  • Profit and loss statements
  • Employment verification
  • Personal income documentation of any kind

Self-employed investors: If you've been declined for conventional loans because your tax returns show low income after deductions, DSCR is specifically designed for your situation. Your personal income is completely irrelevant to the approval decision.

How to Calculate Your DSCR Before Applying

1

Get your rent estimate

Use Rentometer, Zillow rent estimate, or get a letter from a local property manager. For existing rentals, use the current lease amount.

2

Estimate your monthly payment (PITIA)

Use our DSCR calculator at mmehome.com. Include principal + interest + estimated taxes (1.25% of value ÷ 12) + insurance (~$150/mo) + HOA if applicable.

3

Divide rent by PITIA

Rent ÷ PITIA = your DSCR. If it's 0.75 or above, you have a path to approval. If it's 1.0+, you'll have the most lender options and best rates.

4

Submit to multiple lenders simultaneously

A wholesale broker submits your scenario to multiple DSCR lenders at once, finding the one with the best rate and most flexible guidelines for your specific DSCR ratio and property type.

How to Improve a Low DSCR

If your DSCR is below 1.0, here are strategies to improve it before applying:

Increase your down payment

A larger down payment means a smaller loan, which means a lower monthly payment, which improves your DSCR. Going from 20% to 30% down on a $600,000 property reduces the loan by $60,000 and improves your ratio significantly.

Buy down your rate

Paying points to get a lower interest rate reduces your monthly payment and improves your DSCR. This makes sense if you plan to hold the property long-term.

Find a higher-rent property

If you're in the property search phase, filtering for properties with higher rent-to-price ratios will naturally produce better DSCR ratios. Markets outside major coastal cities often have better DSCR ratios.

Use a 40-year amortization

Some DSCR lenders offer 40-year amortization, which reduces the monthly payment compared to a 30-year term. This can push a 0.85 DSCR up to 1.0.

DSCR Loans Through LLC — What You Need to Know

Most real estate investors prefer to hold properties in an LLC for liability protection. DSCR lenders are set up for this — unlike conventional lenders who typically require you to personally sign. Requirements for LLC vesting:

  • Articles of Organization or Incorporation
  • Operating Agreement
  • Certificate of Good Standing (issued within 90 days)
  • EIN documentation
  • Personal guarantee from member(s) with 20%+ ownership

The personal guarantee means your personal credit still matters, but your personal income does not.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a DSCR loan with no rental history on the property?

Yes. For properties without existing leases, lenders use a market rent analysis — typically from an appraiser or a tool like Rentometer — to determine the qualifying rental income. You don't need a tenant in place to apply.

Does a DSCR loan affect my personal debt-to-income ratio?

The DSCR loan itself will appear on your credit report as a liability. If you later apply for a conventional mortgage, lenders may include it in your DTI calculation. However, rental income from the property is also counted as income to offset it.

Can I do a cash-out refinance on an existing property with a DSCR loan?

Yes. DSCR loans are available for purchase and refinance, including cash-out refinance. The same DSCR ratio requirements apply — the property's rent must cover the new, larger payment at the required ratio.

How long does a DSCR loan take to close?

Typically 14–21 days through wholesale lenders. Faster than conventional investment loans because there's no income verification waiting period.
